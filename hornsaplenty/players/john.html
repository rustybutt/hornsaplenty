John Kinney

As a toddler, John's child care provider was a whole family of musicians. Clearly a bad influence that has stayed with to this day.

When John Kinney walked into his first band class, in 5th grade at Kate Kennedy Grammar School, his band teacher, Frank Hansberry, took one look at him and said, "You can't reach 7th position, but I'll teach you anyway. You're playing trombone!" His mother had high hopes for John, but he became a musician anyway.

John and Russ first met in the 7th grade at James Lick Junior High, and have been friends and musical colleagues for half a century. The president of the United States was a guy named John Kennedy, Willie Mays patrolled the outfield for the San Francisco Giants, a ride on Muni cost 15 cents, and if you wanted to subscribe to home delivery of a newspaper, you had a choice of either the Chronicle or the Examiner in the morning, or the News Call Bullitin in the afternoon. Laughing Sal still ruled in the Funhouse at Playland at the Beach and comic books cost a dime.

John, a San Francisco native, currently plays with the Golden Gate Park Band, Paul Price's Society Orchestra, Symphony Parnassus, as well as Horns a Plenty.

John studied with Lewis Van Haney at Indiana University and Edward Herman at the Juilliard School of Music, where he received a Masters degree in trombone performance.

When John returned to San Francisco, he spent several years in the Freeway Philharmonic performing in Napa, Vallejo, Modesto and Walnut Creek. Still driving for a living, John owned and managed a local delivery company, Matrix Express, from 1988 to 2002.

John lives in San Francisco, in the house he grew up in, just blocks away from James Lick Middle School. He resides there with his wife, Luisa, their son, Jack, and their two noisy dogs.

John now pursues a part-time career in music full-time.
